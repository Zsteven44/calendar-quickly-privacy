Calendar Quickly

Privacy Policy

Effective July 15, 2026

Calendar Quickly has no developer-operated server, advertising system, or analytics service. It accesses page information only after you explicitly invoke its context-menu action.

Information the extension accesses

When you select text and choose “Add to Google Calendar,” the extension may access:

  • the text you selected;
  • nearby page text and headings;
  • the page title and URL;
  • event metadata embedded in the page; and
  • your browser’s local time zone.

How the information is used

The extension processes this information locally to infer an event title, date, time, location, and description. It then creates an HTTPS Google Calendar event-template URL and opens it in a new tab so that you can review and complete the event.

Sharing and disclosure

When you invoke the extension, the inferred event information and source link are included in the Google Calendar URL. Google therefore receives that information under the Google Privacy Policy. Calendar Quickly does not send the information to the extension developer or any developer-operated server.

Storage and retention

Calendar Quickly does not store, retain, sell, or use selected text, page content, source URLs, or event information for advertising, profiling, or analytics.

Permissions

  • contextMenus adds the user-invoked selection menu item.
  • activeTab grants temporary access to the tab where you invoke it.
  • scripting extracts relevant information from that tab locally.
